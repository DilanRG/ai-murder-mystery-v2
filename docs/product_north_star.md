# Product North Star

The working Ashwick vertical slice is a preserved foundation and regression
fixture, not the final interpretation of the product. It must remain playable
while the procedural system is developed alongside it.

Given one validated location package, eight selected Character Card V3 cards,
and a seed, the system constructs a new canonical and uniquely solvable
closed-circle murder. One NPC is the victim, seven remain alive as autonomous
agents, and the player detective is separate.

Each living NPC receives only its own case overlay, observations, beliefs,
secrets, relationships, goals, and runtime memory. During play, NPCs may
independently move, converse, investigate, conceal or disclose information,
approach the player, assist, misdirect, and react to world events. They may
propose actions but never author world truth. The authoritative engine validates
every action and judges the final evidence-supported accusation.

## Next vertical milestone

1. Preserve and tag the current authored-case build.
2. Add a structured procedural case schema and generation pipeline.
3. Permit a provider to propose schema-constrained case candidates.
4. Validate timeline consistency, evidence provenance, character knowledge,
   means/motive/opportunity, clue accessibility, and a unique best-supported
   culprit.
5. Compile accepted candidates into immutable canonical truth.
6. Initialize seven NPC agents with genuinely distinct private states and goals.
7. Allow autonomous movement, conversations, information exchange,
   investigation, approach-to-player behaviour, concealment, and bounded
   murderer counterplay.
8. Keep model output behind controlled action and dialogue interfaces.
9. Add deterministic seeded replay and a complete post-game
   truth/action/knowledge audit.
10. Make final accusation evaluate culprit, motive, method, timeline,
    contradictions, and selected evidence.

## Acceptance

At least one mystery must be generated from an arbitrary compatible cast and
location rather than either fixed authored crime spine. It must remain solvable
after autonomous NPC activity and expose at least two independent evidentiary
routes to the uniquely best-supported culprit.

The two authored Ashwick cases and their cast-projection tests remain regression
fixtures. Passing them does not satisfy this procedural acceptance criterion.
