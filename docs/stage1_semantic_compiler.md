# Stage 1 Semantic Plan and Host Compiler

Revision 11 replaces the oversized model-authored canonical Stage 1 document with a compact semantic boundary. This is a Stage 1 qualification change only; the existing Stage 2A/2B/2C ownership split and whole-case validator remain the next boundary.

## Responsibility boundary

The engine first derives a homicide role assignment deterministically from the selected eight Character Card V3 IDs and seed. It locks the victim, responsible actor, and discoverer and exposes them to the model only through compact character aliases.

The model proposes narrative meaning:

- motive and causal rationale;
- a case-specific means concept, origin, access path, and mechanism;
- ordered causal beats with proposed rooms and time windows;
- responsible-actor access, opportunity, cover behaviour, death, and discovery;
- one post-discovery placement for each survivor;
- explicit method, motive, and opportunity support anchors;
- only the relationship and secondary-secret seeds needed by downstream stages.

The host compiles mechanical structure:

- canonical case, means, event, and fact IDs;
- internal event/fact categories and reference links;
- exact monotonic timestamps within the proposed windows;
- role mappings, complete participant records, and survivor map;
- the proof-support catalogue and deterministic fingerprints.

Compilation rejects missing semantic meaning. It does not manufacture a motive, repair a causal story, add a missing support anchor, or choose substitute roles.

## Causal validation

Every causal beat declares dependencies and its role in the chain. Validation checks actor and room aliases, object or means access, topology and travel time, dependency ordering, victim encounter with the means, death after the responsible action, discovery after death, survivor completeness, and all three support axes.

Co-location is event-specific. A direct attack requires responsible-actor and victim co-location at the attack beat. Poison, traps, or other delayed mechanisms may instead bind the responsible actor to preparation or delivery and the victim to a later exposure, so the responsible actor need not be present at death.

The schema can represent suicide by making the victim and responsible actor the same person, and a provider-free validator fixture covers it. Production role selection and runtime behavior remain homicide-only.

## Repair and qualification

Provider/transport failure, output-length stop, empty output, malformed JSON, schema-invalid JSON, semantic rejection, and host-compilation failure are recorded separately. Apparently complete malformed JSON may receive one syntax-only repair. A parsed semantic candidate may receive at most two replace-only deltas tied to its exact fingerprint and issue-authorized paths. Repairs cannot change roles or any undeclared field.

The frozen qualification manifest uses Ashwick Manor as in-world sample content, one eight-card cast, seed `2026072201`, direct DeepSeek with no fallback, Flash before Pro, at most three initial attempts per model, and at most two semantic delta repairs per parsed candidate. It stops after each model independently produces a compiled Stage 1 and valid proof-support catalogue for the existing Stage 2 boundary. Stage 2 provider generation is outside this qualification.
