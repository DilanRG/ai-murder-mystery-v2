# Stage 2 Semantic Evidence Compiler

Stage 2 consumes one provenance-verified, immutable compiled Stage 1 artifact and stops at a fingerprinted Stage 3 input. It does not create overlays, presentation, NPC runtime state, or a playable case.

## Authority boundary

The generation brain owns semantic meaning: route theses, reasoning chains, evidence concepts and causal manifestations, discovery circumstances, alternative interpretations, red-herring suspicion, and innocent explanations.

The engine owns the fixed qualification policy, accepted Stage 1 provenance, opaque aliases, canonical IDs and references, internal categories, exact source events and times, placement slots, executable actions, prerequisite and critical-dependency graphs, implication bookkeeping, fingerprints, validation, invalidation, and admission to the later Stage 3 boundary. A compiler never invents missing causal meaning.

The qualification policy is one immutable object:

- exactly two true routes;
- exactly one primary method, motive, and opportunity/timeline role per route;
- six true evidence roles total;
- exactly two red herrings;
- at least one complete route with no voluntary-disclosure dependency;
- explicit Stage 3 obligations for testimonial access;
- one uniquely best-supported responsible actor at complete Stage 2 knowledge.

## Baseline failure taxonomy

The preserved Revision 7 and Revision 9 results are immutable regression evidence.

| Failure class | Preserved evidence | New control |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Invalid discovery grammar | Revision 7 repeatedly emitted unsupported routes and mismatched slot/object searches | Provider selects only a fingerprinted executable affordance alias; host compiles the exact action |
| Unsupported provenance | Revision 7 evidence referenced undeclared facts or failed timeline closure | Each role is bound to a host-private fact/event projection; Stage 2B cannot author replacements |
| Wrong-axis proof | Revision 7/9 method, motive, and opportunity links used wrong fact categories | Stage 2A aliases have one host-owned axis and wrong-axis selection fails locally |
| Non-grounded route claims | Revision 9 freely copied facts that were not contained in its declared event or did not concern the culprit | Provider sees safe summaries and opaque aliases; host resolves exact Stage 1 references |
| Logical gaps and missing culprit support | Revision 9 routes lacked one axis, timeline support, or a locked-actor link | Each route has three required role briefs, a three-step reasoning chain, a combined inference, and limitations |
| False independence | Revisions 7/9 reused evidence, redundancy groups, causal channels, or discovery dependencies | Host compiles a critical-dependency graph and tests removal of actions, witnesses, containers, access items, prerequisites, and evidence |
| Oversized/malformed output | Revision 7 included over-count evidence documents, truncation, empty output, and malformed JSON | Substage-only semantic schemas use 5k/7k/4k output caps; truncation is never patched; syntax and semantic repair are separate |

These results remain failures. The new contract does not reinterpret or manually complete them.

## Stage 2A — proof-route semantics

The host derives a fingerprinted proof-support catalogue from the accepted Stage 1 facts and containing events. The provider view contains only aliases, safe semantic summaries, axis, causal-beat summary, and permitted evidence channels. Canonical fact IDs, event IDs, room IDs, and actor IDs remain host-private.

The model returns exactly two route proposals, each with a thesis, explicit method/motive/opportunity reasoning, six total role briefs, proposed evidence channel and discovery mode, combined inference, limitations, and independence rationale. The host assigns route/role IDs and binds each role to immutable Stage 1 support.

Validation rejects unknown/stale aliases, wrong-axis support, unsupported channels, incomplete reasoning, testimonial-access misclassification, identical route channel patterns, and shared truth presented through the same manifestation channel.

## Stage 2B — evidence and discovery

The discovery-affordance catalogue is derived from location topology, searchable objects, evidence slots, item-gated access, the body action, living interview targets, and accepted events. It tells the provider where and how evidence may be found without prescribing the evidence concept.

For every immutable role ref, the model supplies its preserved concept and channel, causal origin, manifestation delay, persistence, involved actor aliases, one offered discovery affordance, circumstances, meaningful prerequisites, support, contradiction, alternatives, limits, and preservation semantics.

The host assigns evidence IDs, source facts/events, exact time, actor IDs, room/slot placement, exact player action, prerequisite evidence IDs, route membership, redundancy identity, and any Stage 3 testimonial obligation. Validation checks chronology, actor presence, form/placement compatibility, executable access, prerequisite closure/cycles, route coverage, immutable Stage 2A ownership, and at least one non-voluntary route.

Independence is mechanical. For every route the engine constructs critical nodes for evidence, prerequisites, discovery affordances, actions, containers, access items, and witnesses. Removing a node may disable one route, but no node may disable both. The same witness therefore cannot be a mandatory bottleneck for both routes.

## Stage 2C — truthful misdirection

The host derives an opaque catalogue of innocent secondary-secret seeds from Stage 1. The model creates exactly two red herrings, each tied to an offered innocent owner and seed, with a bounded secondary-event window and participants, suspicious evidence, causal source, executable discovery, reasonable misinterpretation, non-murder explanation, contradiction hook, and executable resolution.

The host fits each proposed innocent event into the existing actor timeline and room graph, then owns its exact ID, time, room, fact link, and references. It also owns red-herring evidence IDs, exact provenance, placement/actions, implication/exoneration bookkeeping, and uniqueness scoring. Stage 2C cannot mutate Stage 1, Stage 2A, or Stage 2B. A red herring may not enter a true route or make an innocent actor equally best supported.

## Repair and phase-aware admission

Every accepted candidate is normalized and fingerprinted. A semantic repair receives the actual candidate, stable issue codes and paths, immutable paths, and only issue-authorized replace paths. Stale fingerprints, immutable-stage changes, and undeclared paths fail closed. There are at most three initial attempts per substage and two deltas per parsed rejected candidate.

Validation has five explicit entry points: Stage 2A, Stage 2B, Stage 2C, assembled Stage 1 plus Stage 2 evidence, and Stage 3 readiness. The assembled gate reuses the unchanged evidence/solution validator. It does not manufacture fake overlays or presentation.

Complete at Stage 2: route logic and coverage, provenance, executable or deferred discovery, prerequisite closure, critical-dependency independence, red-herring causality/resolution, full-information culprit uniqueness, and fingerprints.

Deferred legitimately to Stage 3: witness beliefs, concealment, lies, disclosure policy, persuasion timing, and dialogue phrasing. Each critical testimonial has an explicit obligation and is not counted as already accessible.

Deferred to Stage 4: public prose and leak checking. Full-case admission remains a later gate after real overlays and presentation exist.
