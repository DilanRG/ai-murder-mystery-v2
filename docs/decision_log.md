# AI Murder Mystery Game — Decision Log

Short, dated records of settled product and architecture choices. This log records decisions, not implementation status or future work. The [Product North Star](product_north_star.md) defines the invariant product intent, the [Game Design Roadmap](AI_Murder_Mystery_Game_Design_Roadmap.md) owns sequencing and acceptance gates, and [Active Build Status](active_status.md) records what is currently verified.

Add a new entry when a decision is settled or superseded; do not silently rewrite an older entry. Link the replacement from the earlier entry when practical.

## 2026-07-21

- **Product identity:** The product is **AI Murder Mystery Game**. “Ashwick” is reserved for intentional in-world sample content and compatibility identifiers, not the product, application, or overall build.
- **Cast boundary:** A new game selects eight NPC Character Card V3 cards. One is the victim and seven remain alive; the player detective is separate from all eight cards.
- **Murderer role:** Exactly one of the seven living NPCs is the murderer. The other six living NPCs are innocent suspects, though they may still conceal unrelated secrets or mislead the player.
- **Authority:** The engine owns canonical truth, time, topology, evidence, perception, action resolution, and the final verdict. Models may propose bounded actions and portray dialogue, but may not author or patch world truth.
- **State separation:** Canonical truth, world state, observations, beliefs, claims, and player knowledge are distinct data layers. A statement does not become a fact merely because an NPC or model emitted it.
- **Character overlays:** Reusable character cards provide identity and personality. Case roles, private observations, beliefs, secrets, relationships, goals, and runtime memory belong to case-specific overlays.
- **Case admission:** A generated candidate becomes immutable canonical truth only after schema, chronology, provenance, knowledge, means/motive/opportunity, accessibility, and unique-culprit validation passes.
- **Solvability:** Every admitted procedural case must retain at least two independent evidentiary routes to one uniquely best-supported culprit. Bounded murderer counterplay may not eliminate the final viable route.
- **Authored fixtures:** Existing authored Ashwick cases remain playable regression fixtures. They do not count as procedural-case acceptance evidence.
- **Play modes:** Turn-based play remains supported as a first-class mode. Continuous event-driven simulation may become a later selectable mode; it does not replace the turn-based foundation.
- **Player visibility:** Player-facing interfaces receive only a perception-filtered projection. Maps, APIs, dialogue, saves, and UI state must not leak canonical truth or private NPC state during play.
- **Testing standard:** Tests must be falsifiable and adversarial. Procedural acceptance also requires blind playthroughs through the actual player interface without source, debug, save-file, or hidden-state access.
- **Phase 1 gate:** The procedural engine has passed its first technical generated-case gate. Phase 1 remains active until three materially varied procedural cases pass validation, two blind interface-only agent playthroughs reach fair supported accusations on different cases, authored regressions remain green, and at least one human blind playthrough is completed.
- **Provider testing hold:** Real-provider playtesting remains paused until the project owner lifts the hold. Dummy providers exercise the production admission boundary without spending API credits meanwhile.
- **Provider direction:** OpenRouter is the current generation adapter, not a permanent product dependency. A later provider abstraction should support major APIs and compatible local endpoints without placing provider logic in the engine.
- **Difficulty model:** Difficulty is multi-dimensional and should be expressed through named profiles composed from subsystem presets, with advanced custom controls later.
- **Location authoring:** A validated room graph is the authoritative first representation of a location package. A 2D map is a later perception-filtered view over that graph, not a second source of truth.
- **Forensics sequencing:** Forensic and DFIR systems are deferred until the core mystery game is proven and the forensic collaborator has completed a blind playtest.
- **Controlled real-provider exception:** The real-provider testing hold is lifted only for the budget-capped DeepSeek V4 Pro/Flash evaluation. The experiment has a USD 8.50 soft stop, USD 9.50 operational hard stop, and USD 0.50 accounting reserve; all other real-provider playtesting remains paused.
- **Two-phase agent acceptance:** Agent acceptance is split into Phase A blind intended play and Phase B adversarial black-box play. Phase B begins only after Phase A passes, and post-fix normal play must follow adversarial fixes.

## 2026-07-22

- **OpenRouter evaluation route:** The DeepSeek V4 comparison uses the OpenRouter endpoint and the exact `deepseek/deepseek-v4-pro` or `deepseek/deepseek-v4-flash` model slug, matching the proven legacy-v2 integration. OpenRouter may choose or fail over between compatible serving providers; no fallback model is configured or accepted. Every successful response must report the exact requested model, serving provider, accounting mode, and inclusive external charge. This supersedes the experiment-only decision to require a direct DeepSeek BYOK endpoint.
- **DeepSeek upstream clarification:** This supersedes the preceding OpenRouter-route interpretation. Requests still enter through OpenRouter, but the experiment must force the `deepseek` upstream provider, use the owner's prioritized DeepSeek BYOK key, disable shared/provider fallback, and require `is_byok=true`. The measured adapter omits `top_k` because the direct V4 endpoints do not support it; the ordinary product adapter retains its legacy default.
- **Direct DeepSeek evaluation fallback:** This supersedes both preceding experiment-routing entries after OpenRouter confirmed that account privacy/guardrail policy excluded the forced DeepSeek endpoint despite a valid routing payload. Experiment revision 4 uses the official direct DeepSeek API, exact `deepseek-v4-pro` and `deepseek-v4-flash` IDs, no fallback, and complete token-meter accounting against the frozen official price card. The normal product's OpenRouter path remains supported and unchanged by this experiment-only transport choice.
- **Reasoning/output budget:** A generation response must have room for both private reasoning and the final schema document. After revision-4 Pro exhausted 16,384 tokens entirely on reasoning, revision 5 raises the case-generation ceiling to 32,768 for both Pro and Flash; the paired comparison may not give one model a larger allowance. The measured transport enforces frozen role limits at the final request boundary so a lower production-adapter default cannot silently override the manifest.
- **Staged canonical generation:** Revision 6 supersedes the one-shot generation contract after three complete-looking Flash candidates all failed global admission. Generation is split into crime/facts, evidence/solution, private overlays, and public presentation. Every stage is schema-validated and repaired locally; assembled truth must still pass the unchanged authoritative global validator before it becomes canonical, and presentation runs only after truth admission.
- **Prompt-cache boundary:** All generation stages and repairs share a byte-identical system/context prefix. Stage instructions, accepted upstream artifacts, and mutable repair feedback are appended after it so automatic provider prefix caching can reduce repeated input cost without changing authority or validation rules. Cache hits are measured, never assumed.
- **Context-window setting:** The unused 8,192-token local-hosting preference is removed instead of pretending to constrain hosted providers. Provider-specific context discovery and enforcement belongs in the later provider-abstraction layer; the staged generator currently relies on each configured provider's advertised limit and its own bounded output ceilings.
- **External evaluation disclosure:** The owner explicitly authorizes the budget-capped evaluation to send the frozen Character Card V3 inputs, location package, schemas, prompts, and accepted-stage artifacts to the official external DeepSeek API. This does not authorize unrelated provider traffic or public disclosure.
- **Interrupted-cell policy:** A controller interruption never permits an invisible retry. The affected cell and unresolved accounting remain in the audit denominator; revision 6 P1 is operationally invalidated, and the predeclared R1 reserve replaces it symmetrically for both models under revision 7.
- **Durable evaluation plan:** Revision 7 may execute only P2, P3, and R1, with R1 marked as replacing P1. The manifest digest, plan, current cell, pre-transport request intent, responses, stage outcomes, and completed cells are fsync-persisted around their corresponding boundaries. An incomplete or missing plan with a revision-7 generation intent fails closed instead of automatically resending a paid request.
- **Experiment cost terminology:** Provider dashboard charges and locally settled requests are actual observed spend. Open ledger reservations are pessimistic worst-case exposure holds for safety enforcement and must never be reported as billed cost.
- **Revision 8 evidence-stage granularity:** Revision 7 admitted zero of six cases because its Stage 2 contract combined evidence inventory and solution routing under inconsistent item/route bounds and implicit discovery grammar. The four conceptual ownership phases remain; Stage 2 now returns two immutable provider deltas: exactly eight evidence items (six route supports and two red herrings), then exactly two solution routes over that accepted inventory. The host assembles the unchanged production Stage 2 schema and runs the unchanged whole-case validator. This changes provider-stage granularity only; it does not weaken engine truth, stage ownership, fairness controls, or final admission.
- **Revision 9 responsibility split:** Revision 8 is preserved as a superseded partial experiment after two settled P2/Pro core attempts and one operationally unresolved request. Stage 2 is now three immutable deltas: 2A proves two abstract, independent routes from accepted Stage 1 facts/events; 2B realizes their six concrete evidence roles with canonical provenance and executable discovery; 2C adds two causally explained red herrings and connective structure. The host owns only deterministic IDs, reference normalization, and action compilation. The unchanged assembled evidence and whole-case admission validators remain authoritative.
- **Revision 7 Stage 1 reuse:** Accepted Revision 7 Stage 1 outputs may not be reused because the immutable archive retains their dispositions and provider metrics but not the normalized documents and exact content fingerprints required to prove identity. Revision 9 must regenerate Stage 1 rather than reconstruct or manually edit truth.
- **Revision 9 empirical disposition:** The exact-commit responsibility-split matrix is immutable with zero admissions. P2/Flash exhausted Stage 1; the other five cells accepted Stage 1 and exhausted Stage 2A. This is a validator rejection result, not permission to weaken validation or reinterpret partial stages as cases.
- **Revision 10 proof-support catalog:** Stage 2A may select only opaque entries from a fingerprinted catalog mechanically derived from accepted Stage 1 facts and their containing events. The host may normalize those references and compile timeline facts, but the model continues to own causal claims, evidence forms, route pairing, and independence rationale. Existing downstream and whole-case validators remain unchanged.
- **Accepted-stage provenance:** New measured generation cells privately persist each accepted provider delta and the host-compiled proof blueprint with content fingerprints and request/model provenance. Earlier Stage 1 output may be reused only when that exact state and provenance are present; truth may never be reconstructed from metrics or manually repaired outside the declared pipeline.
- **Seeded engine-owned death roles:** Before Stage 1 generation, the engine deterministically selects and locks the death mode, victim, responsible actor, and discoverer from the chosen eight cards and seed. The model acknowledges aliases for those roles but may neither choose nor repair them. The current production path remains homicide-only.
- **Semantic Stage 1 responsibility split:** The model owns a compact semantic crime plan: motive, causal rationale, case-specific means, event beats, proposed rooms and timing, cover behaviour, discovery, survivor placements, and explicit method/motive/opportunity anchors. The host owns canonical IDs, internal enums, exact normalized times, references, fingerprints, mechanical facts/events, and the case-specific means record; it may not invent missing narrative meaning or support.
- **Causal death-chain validation:** Semantic Stage 1 validation follows preparation, delivery or placement, victim encounter, injury/death, concealment, and discovery dependencies. This replaces universal culprit-victim co-location at the death minute. A direct attack still requires co-location at the attack event, while a valid delayed mechanism requires responsible access earlier in its chain.
- **Suicide-compatible representation only:** The semantic role and causal-chain representation can express a victim who is also the responsible actor, and a focused validator fixture proves this. New Story selection, NPC initialization, counterplay, accusation logic, and player-facing suicide support remain deferred.
- **Comprehension-first Stage 1 prompting:** Stage 1 receives a small authority prefix, only the semantic-plan schema, only required cast/location context, locked role aliases, explicit acceptance rules, and one concise example. Stage 2-4 schemas are excluded even when doing so reduces cache-prefix reuse.
- **Fingerprinted semantic delta repair:** A parsed semantic candidate is normalized and fingerprinted before repair. Repair returns only replace operations for issue-declared paths against that exact fingerprint; stale fingerprints, locked-role changes, and undeclared paths fail closed. Malformed complete JSON has a separate bounded syntax repair, while truncated output is never treated as patchable.
- **Stage 2 semantic authority:** Stage 2 providers author proof and evidence meaning through opaque aliases. The engine owns catalogues, canonical references, placement, executable actions, dependency graphs, scoring, fingerprints, validation, and the later admission decision.
- **Fixed Stage 2 qualification policy:** The current qualification requires exactly two true routes, six primary evidence roles, two causally explained red herrings, at least one fully non-voluntary route, explicit Stage 3 testimonial obligations, and one uniquely best-supported responsible actor. This is not a general difficulty-profile system.
- **Mechanical route independence:** Routes may share background Stage 1 truth, but no critical evidence, prerequisite, witness, container, action, access item, or discovery dependency may disable both. Model-written independence rationale is necessary but never sufficient without the host removal proof.
- **Stage 2 stop boundary:** Successful Stage 2 qualification produces a fingerprinted candidate valid for Stage 3. It does not create fake overlays or presentation, admit a full case, initialize NPCs, or begin playtesting.
- **Explicit direct-DeepSeek thinking control:** DeepSeek V4 defaults to thinking mode even when no reasoning effort is supplied. Direct preflight and repair requests therefore send `thinking: disabled`; semantic initial requests continue to send `thinking: enabled` with high effort. The first eight-token Flash preflight that exposed this was an accounted operational failure, not a model-quality rejection.
- **Stage 2A realization feasibility:** An abstract proof blueprint is accepted only if the host can allocate all six roles to executable affordances without a critical dependency shared across both routes. The provider receives bounded nonzero realization counts, but Stage 2B retains ownership of the concrete evidence and affordance choices. This is the first observed-failure-backed Stage 2 contract revision.
- **Durable Stage 2 substage checkpoints:** Every accepted semantic candidate and host-compiled delta is atomically persisted with exact Git, manifest, model, prompt/schema, and Stage 1 provenance. Resume rebuilds the private catalogues, revalidates the candidate, recompiles it, and requires byte-identical documents and fingerprints before skipping a provider request.
- **Stage 2 discovery ownership:** Stage 2A owns abstract proof support and evidence channels, not concrete discovery modes or affordances. Stage 2B selects an offered executable affordance and the host derives its exact discovery mode, placement, and action. This removes an observed cross-stage ownership conflict without weakening either validator.
- **Route-delta Stage 2A:** The final allowed Stage 2 contract revision generates Route 1 and Route 2 as separate immutable deltas. Route 1 must be fully non-testimonial; Route 2 is bound to Route 1's exact fingerprint. The host accepts the pair only after the unchanged combined coverage, realization-feasibility, and critical-dependency independence proofs pass.
- **Experiment-result identity:** A terminal qualification result is reusable only for the exact experiment revision, clean Git SHA, and frozen manifest fingerprint that produced it. Historical failures remain immutable evidence but cannot suppress a later authorized revision's provider calls.
- **Completed-checkpoint operational recovery:** A completed paid checkpoint may cross a controller-only repair commit only when the source is exact, complete, directly parented, the changed-file set is explicitly bounded, every accepted document is recompiled and revalidated, and the recovery is append-only audited. This rule prevented duplicate Flash semantic calls after a post-validation serialization fault; it does not authorize general cross-revision reuse.
- **Revision 14 empirical disposition:** Flash passed complete Stage 2 and produced a Stage-3-ready artifact. Pro passed Stage 2A and Stage 2B but all three Stage 2C attempts truncated at the fixed output cap. Partial Pro stages are preserved evidence, not a qualification success.
- **Stage 2 revision-limit stop:** Revision 13 and Revision 14 consumed the Goal's two permitted contract-revision cycles. Because both models did not complete Stage 2, this Goal stops cleanly with classified evidence. Any Stage 2C decomposition requires a new owner-authorized Goal; Stage 3 remains blocked until both models hold complete accepted Stage 2 artifacts.
